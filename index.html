Presidential Election Chronicles: Kwara State Trends and Insights

Applied spatial analytics to 2,518 polling units across Kwara State to detect potential voting irregularities in the 2023 Nigerian presidential election. Used DBSCAN for geographic clustering, Local Moran's I (on turnout rate) for spatial autocorrelation, flagging 986 statistically significant units, narrowed to 288 High-High and 245 Low-Low locations when cross-validated against Getis-Ord Gi* hotspot analysis. Combined results with Isolation Forest anomaly detection to produce a normalized composite outlier score, identifying Ajikobi Ward (Ilorin West LGA) and Tsonga 111 (Edu LGA) as the highest-risk areas. Integrated historical election data (2015–2023) and demographic data to contextualise findings, revealing APC's declining dominance and Labour Party's emergence as a third force, with APC-PDP vote margins tightening in most tracked LGAs between 2019 and 2023.

SCGR: Sales, Logistics & Customer Insights Dashboard

Built an end-to-end interactive BI dashboard analysing sales performance, logistics efficiency, and customer satisfaction across 99,000 e-commerce orders. Engineered a relational data model connecting 8 datasets and used DAX/calculated fields for KPI tracking and root-cause analysis, uncovering a 6.77% late delivery rate and showing that shipping delays exceeding five days significantly drop customer review scores. Originally built in Power BI; independently rebuilt in Tableau to reconstruct two AI-driven visuals (decomposition tree, key influencers) with no native Tableau equivalent — the rebuild also surfaced and corrected two measurement errors in the original model. Tools: Power BI, Tableau, Power Query, DAX, Excel.

Predicting Internal Displacement

Analysed internal displacement patterns across five countries to uncover the key drivers of hazard-induced displacement from floods and droughts. Key findings included Somalia recording the highest displacement levels in 2023 driven primarily by flooding, and a clear inverse relationship between GDP and displacement — suggesting economic strength acts as a buffer against disaster impact. Also identified that disaster frequency and low-elevation geography were significant vulnerability indicators. Visualised findings through scatterplots comparing GDP, elevation, and disaster frequency against displacement counts using Python and Seaborn.
